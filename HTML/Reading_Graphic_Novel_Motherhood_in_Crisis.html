The stories of Memunatu, Heela, Kadiatu, and Tetteh all show how people can go through really hard situations because of things they can’t control. What stood out to me is that none of them chose these problems, but they still have to deal with the consequences. Each story is different, but they all connect to bigger issues in society like healthcare, education, safety, and justice not being equal for everyone.
Memunatu’s story, “The distant patient,” really shows how scary it can be to not have access to basic healthcare. She lives far from any hospital, and when she’s about to give birth, she has to travel a long distance just to get help. That’s something that shouldn’t even be a problem, but for her it is. It made me realize how much where you live can affect your life. Something as normal as having a baby becomes dangerous just because she doesn’t have resources nearby.
Heela’s story, “My stolen future,” felt really unfair. She’s just a schoolgirl who should be focused on her education and what she wants to do in life, but instead that gets taken away from her. It shows how some girls don’t get the same chances as others just because of their situation or where they’re from. School is supposed to help you build your future, but for her, that future is cut short. It makes you think about how many people never get the chance to reach their full potential.
Kadiatu’s story, “The devil at my door,” is honestly really sad. She goes through a huge loss as a mother, and it shows how unsafe things can be for some people. Your home is supposed to be the safest place, but for her it turns into a place of fear. It made me think about how not everyone has the same level of protection or security, and how quickly things can go wrong.
Tetteh’s story, “My punishment,” talks about what it’s like being in prison. It made me question how fair the justice system really is. Being locked up doesn’t just take away your freedom, it can take away your identity and hope too. His story shows that once you’re in that system, it’s really hard to get out or start over.
Overall, these stories made me realize that a lot of people struggle because of bigger problems in society, not because of their own choices. Whether it’s lack of healthcare, losing education, dealing with violence, or being stuck in the justice system, all of these things affect people’s lives in serious ways. It just shows that the world isn’t fair for everyone, and there’s a lot that needs to change.
