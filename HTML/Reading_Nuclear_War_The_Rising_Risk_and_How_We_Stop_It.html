In the article “Nuclear War: The Rising Risk, and How We Stop It,” W.J. Hennigan explains how nuclear war is becoming a real threat again, not just something from the past. One line that stood out to me was when he said, “The risk of nuclear conflict is rising.” This shows that things are actually getting more dangerous today, even though a lot of people don’t really think about nuclear war anymore. It feels like people assume it won’t happen, but the article proves that it’s still a serious issue.

Hennigan also talks about how countries are starting to build up their nuclear weapons again, saying they are “speeding toward the next arms race.” This reminded me of the Cold War, where countries kept trying to outdo each other with more powerful weapons. If that keeps happening, it makes sense that the chances of a nuclear war would go up. The more weapons there are, the more likely it is that one could be used, especially during a conflict.

Another important part is when the article says, “It hangs over battlefields in Ukraine as well as places where the next war might occur.” This made it feel way more real to me because it’s not just talking about random places, it's talking about actual conflicts happening right now or tensions that could turn into war. Places like Ukraine, the Taiwan Strait, and the Korean Peninsula are all mentioned. If a war breaks out in these areas, there’s a chance nuclear weapons could be used, especially if one side feels like they’re losing and want to quickly change the situation.

The article also explains that today’s nuclear weapons are smaller but still extremely deadly, saying they are “magnitudes more deadly than conventional ones.” This is important because it means even a “small” nuclear weapon could cause massive destruction. It also makes them seem more usable in war, which is actually more dangerous. If leaders think they can use a smaller nuclear weapon without causing total destruction, they might be more willing to use it, which could start a chain reaction.

Another quote that really stood out was, “The possibility of a nuclear strike… is more likely now than at any other time since the Cold War.” This shows how serious the situation is right now. It’s not just a small risk, it's one of the highest it’s been in a long time. The fact that governments are already preparing for this kind of situation makes it even more real.

Overall, I think the article is trying to warn people that if another big war happens, it could easily turn into a nuclear war. There are more countries with nuclear weapons, tensions are growing, and the systems that used to control these weapons are breaking down. If even one country uses a nuclear weapon, others might respond, and it could keep escalating. That’s what makes this so dangerous. It’s not just about one bomb it’s about how quickly things could get out of control. This article made me realize that nuclear war isn’t impossible, and if people keep ignoring it, it could actually happen.
